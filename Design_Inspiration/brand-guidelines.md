# Founder Copilot — Brand Guidelines and Design System

## 1. Brand intent
Founder Copilot should not look like another safe, polished, anonymous SaaS landing page.
It should feel: sharp, tense, intelligent, slightly dangerous, founder-native, emotionally charged, memorable on first open.
The reaction should be: **“This feels unusual. Maybe even wrong. But I can’t stop looking.”**
Goal: controlled intensity. Not childish. Not random. Not ugly for the sake of ugly.

## 2. Core brand feeling
- **Emotional territory**: urgency, obsession, signal over noise, late-night founder energy, internet-native sharpness, creative tension, anti-corporate clarity.
- **Not this**: default B2B SaaS blue gradient, sterile enterprise dashboard, generic AI glow product, calming meditation productivity tool, polished startup template clone.
- **Internal positioning**: a founder war-room, not a software brochure

## 3. Brand personality
- **Traits**: opinionated, intense, high taste, clear, experimental, anti-generic, strategic, internet-aware
- **Voice**: ships fast, thinks sharply, hates generic marketing, sees signal others miss, is building in public with intent

## 4. Creative direction
**Signal vs Noise**
The entire brand should feel like it is pulling signal out of chaos. Tension, contrast, distortion in controlled places, dense moments next to open space, strong editorial typography, almost poster-like hero sections, brutal emphasis elements.

## 5. Color strategy
- Primary background: **Obsidian Black** `#0A0A0B`
- Secondary background: **Smoke Charcoal** `#151518`
- Primary text: **Bone White** `#F5F1E8`
- Secondary text: **Dust Gray** `#A8A29A`
- Primary accent: **Acid Lime** `#C6FF2E` (signal, danger, attention, activation)
- Secondary accent: **Hot Vermilion** `#FF5A36` (friction, urgency, heat, disruption)
- Tertiary accent: **Electric Violet** `#8B5CF6` (intelligence, weirdness, system depth)
- Optional accent: **Toxic Cyan** `#37F0FF` (digital signal, machine energy)

## 6. Color behavior rules
1. Dark mode is the main brand environment.
2. Bright accents as interruptions, not wallpaper.
3. One dominant accent per section.
4. Mostly dark, neutral, typographic — with bursts of chemical color.
5. Avoid soft pastel gradients and soft glows everywhere.

## 7. Typography direction
- **Display font**: Editorial serif with attitude (e.g. Instrument Serif, Playfair) or highly compressed grotesk (e.g. Space Grotesk, Oswald).
- **Body font**: Modern grotesk, clean but characterful (e.g. Inter, Space Grotesk).
- **Mono / system accent font**: Monospace for metadata, labels, system states (e.g. Space Mono, JetBrains Mono).

## 8. Typography behavior rules
- **Hero**: oversized, bold, tightly kerned, dramatic line breaks, asymmetrical.
- **Body**: highly readable, moderate line length, clean hierarchy.
- **Contrast**: mix huge statement typography with small technical labels.
- **Avoid**: soft rounded fonts, overly friendly brand typography.

## 9. Layout philosophy
- Use tension, break the grid intentionally, distinct section moods, interruption moments, keep conversion clarity.

## 10. Visual elements
- **Use**: oversized type blocks, monospace labels, directional arrows, harsh underlines, thin rule lines and boxed callouts, layered text blocks, poster-style titles, signal markers, micro-annotations, brutal CTA buttons, high-contrast cards.
- **Avoid**: floating 3D blobs, generic AI sparkles, soft glassmorphism, overused blue-purple startup gradients, stock illustrations of teamwork, cartoon mascots, generic dashboard mockups, soft rounded everything.

## 11. Practical brand system recommendation
**Dark editorial brutality with electric signal accents.**
- dark background, warm white type, acid lime or hot vermilion accents, dramatic hero typography, sans for body, monospace for system layer, broken-grid editorial composition, poster energy, interface fragments as proof.

## 12. Extreme but controlled rule
The site should feel like a **risky design decision that somehow still converts.**
Someone with very strong taste got tired of boring SaaS and made something with teeth.
