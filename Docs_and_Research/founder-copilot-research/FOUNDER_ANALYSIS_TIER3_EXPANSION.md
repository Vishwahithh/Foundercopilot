## New Patterns from Tier 3

### 1. Format Diversity Matters
**Finding:** Tier 3 is highly format-diverse. Some are video-first (Niki, Dickie), some are visual (Julia), some are essay-first (Sahil), some are data-first (Hiten). No one-size-fits-all approach.

**Implication for Founder Copilot:**
- Need format-preference learning (YouTube script ≠ Twitter thread ≠ Zine outline)
- Platform/audience determines format, not just content

### 2. Vertical-Specific Language Matters
**Finding:** Technical creators (Swyx, Julia) use jargon + explanation. B2B operators (Toni) use frameworks + business language. Creators (Dickie, Niki) use hype + psychological triggers.

**Implication for Founder Copilot:**
- Language register varies by vertical
- Founder Copilot needs to learn vertical-specific voice patterns

### 3. Rare/High-Stakes Communication Has Different Rules
**Finding:** Daniel Ek (rare tweets) and Anne-Laure (evidence-based) operate under higher accuracy/tone constraints. More content ≠ better. Quality + credibility is paramount.

**Implication for Founder Copilot:**
- Some founders need *curation* help (what NOT to publish) more than generation help
- High-stakes communication needs higher accuracy bars
